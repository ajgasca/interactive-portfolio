Coding Bootcamp

My Journey So Far

March 12, 2019

I am now about 2/3 of the way through the University of Arizona Coding Bootcamp, and I cannot believe how fast the time has passed since I started back in late October. I remember being inspired by the Professional Services team members at my company. I remember thinking, "how can I be like them?". After a few Google searches for coding bootcamps in the valley, and a lot of sneaky online marketing by U of A, I decided to take a risk I thought I would never do. I chose to take out a loan to join this coding bootcamp not knowing how I would take to web development, how adding a part-time class would complicate my overall life, and having no guarantee that I would be successful. This one of the biggest leaps I have ever ventured.

The first few weeks were engaging enough. While beginning to use HTML and CSS it was thrilling to watch as code written translated to something on a computer screen. Then came Javascript and the pace has not slowed down since. Variables, functions, if/else statements, and for loops are one thing. Closures, scope, and callbacks are something else entirely. As I have grown throughout the program and my Javascript skills have increased, I am feeling more confident in my ability to create applications. Our early front-end projects were a trial by fire, and I enjoyed them immensely. I think the real challenge I have faced has been learning the back-end. Node.js, establishing routes, working with servers and databases, and understanding how all those pieces fit together has been the bulk of the journey. It was only after finishing this second group project that I have felt proud in all the knowledge I have gained through the program thus far.

You can't get there by bus, only by hard work, risking, and by not quite knowing what you're doing. What you'll discover will be wonderful: yourself. --Alan Alda

A year ago I was working as a server at a restaurant wondering if I would ever break the cycle of my life. If you would have asked me if I would change to a full-time job and juggle school at the same time I would have said you are crazy. Some of the weeks these past few months have felt like an eternity. At the other end of this eternity, I can say I am excited for what the future has in store for me once the bootcamp concludes.
